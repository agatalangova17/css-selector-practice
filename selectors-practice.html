Quantum mechanics explores the behavior of matter and energy at the smallest scales. It reveals a world where probabilities replace certainty and where matter has properties of both particles and waves.

General relativity is a theory of gravitation developed by Albert Einstein between 1907 and 1915, aiming to reconcile Newton's law of universal gravitation with the laws of special relativity. It describes gravity not as a force, but as a curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

Physics involves studying forces, energy, and matter to understand how the universe works. From the immense gravitational pull of black holes to the intricate dance of subatomic particles, physics encompasses the broad sweep of the cosmos's mysteries.
